What is Agile?

An agile project is a value driven approach that helps the project manager and development team deliver high priority and high quality work. Software project are always changing throughout a project. An agile managed project will embrace these changes, even late in the development stage. This is because an agile project prioritises the customers needs first, delivering products with the greatest business value first, and always having relevant, real time information to efficiently manage cost, time and s cope.


What are the 12 principles of Agile?

Satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery
Welcome changing requirements, even late in the development stage
Deliver working software frequently
Business people and developers work together and communicate on a daily basis
Build projects around motivated individuals
Convey information via face-to-face conversation
Working software is the primary measure of progress
Maintain a constant pace indefinitely
Give continuous attention to technical excellence
Manage the amount of work not completed
Teams self-organise
Teams retrospect and tune behaviour

What are the roles in an Agile project?

There are many people involved in completing an agile project, most fall into one of the 5 roles below

Development Team

A group of people who essentially create the product. This team is typically made up of programmers, testers, designers writers and anyone else who has a hands on role in the product development

Product Owner

A product owner is the person who the product is primarily being developed for. He is responsible for communication with the customer and delivering information about the customer’s value and needs to the development team. He will work with the development team on a daily basis to clarify requirements from the customer

Scrum master

A scrum master: is responsible for supporting the development team by clearing organisational roadblock and keeping the agile process running smoothly

Stakeholders

A stakeholder is anyone with an interest in the project. They are not responsible for the product once it has been developed, however they provide input and can influence the final outcome of a project

Agile Mentor

A agile mentor is someone who has had previous experience in the implementation of an age project. Because of this they are able to share their experiences with the team and help the project to run more efficiently.

Agile vs Waterfall

Which project management is right for you? Rightly selected project management is the key to success. An effective framework makes the project run smoothly. Between waterfall and agile management, you have you have to make a right choice. Here are some pros and cons that help you make your decision.

Agile

Fast and flexible. Principles of collaboration, adaptability and continuous improvement. Typically set up in quick, iterative project release cycles.

Best for projects that deal with services-oriented and non-physical deliverables.
Allows for quick course correction based on stakeholder feedback.
Empowers project team to work creatively and efficiently.
Includes engagement and collaboration from all team members.
Not suited for projects with strictly defined requirements and scope.
Uncertainty around scope and schedules can make stakeholders and executives nervous.
Requires vigilant backlog and documentation maintenance, and tech debt management.

Waterfall

Detailed planning at the beginning All step laid out, one after the previous is completed

Best for projects that deal with physical objects – from a construction project to a hardware installation project.
Best for projects with defined tasks and phases with a specific sequence.
Project plans are repeatable for identical or similar projects in the future.
Required substantial scope and schedule planning before work begins.
Scope changes can be slow and require formal change control process.
Less effective for software, design and other non-physical or services-based projects.

Benefits of Agile

Benefits of Agile Management

  1. Better product quality
  2. Improved performance visibility
  3. Reduced risk
  4. Handle change
  5. Higher customer satisfaction
  6. Higher team morale
  7. Increased collaboration and ownership
  8. Customized team structures
  9. More relevant metrics
  10. Increased project control
  11. Improved project predictability

Better Product Quality

Agile methods have excellent safeguards to ensure highest quality by: A proactive approach to prevent problems Embracing technological excellence, good design, and sustainable development  Defining and elaborating on requirements just in time so that knowledge of product features is as relevant as possible  Incorporating continuous integration and daily testing into the development process, allowing the development team to address issues while they're fresh Taking advantage of automated testing tools in order to develop during the day, test overnight, and fix bugs in the morning Conducting sprint retrospectives, allowing the scrum team to continuously improve processes and work Completing work using the definition of done: developed, tested, integrated, and documented.

Improved performance visibility

On agile projects, every member of the project team has the opportunity to know how the project is going at any given time. Daily scrum meetings, daily sprint reviews, and visible progress charts offer concrete ways to see progress.

Reduced Risk

Agile techniques virtually eliminate the chance of absolute project failure: Developing in sprints, ensuring a short time between initial project investment and either failing fast or knowing that a product or an approach will work Always having a working product, starting with the very first sprint, so that no agile project fails completely Developing requirements to the definition of done in each sprint so that project sponsors have completed, usable features, regardless of what may happen with the project in the future Providing constant feedback on products and processes through daily scrum meetings and constant development team communication, sprint reviews and retrospectives, and releases in which the end user can see and react to new features on a regular basis Generating revenue early with self-funding projects, allowing organizations to pay for a project with little up-front expense.
